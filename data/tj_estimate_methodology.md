# Trader Joe's Private Estimates — Methodology & Confidence

## Revenue Estimate: $24B (FY2024)

### Triangulation sources
1. **Worldly Partners (Oct 2025):** "Estimated annual revenue approximately over $20 billion" for 2023.
2. **Acquired Briefing (Oct 2025):** "$20B+ as of 2023 (per CEO Dan Bane on podcast); estimated $24-25B for 2024-2025 based on continued ~11% annual growth."
3. **Business Strategy Hub:** $13.3B in FY2022.
4. **Implied CAGR check:** $13.3B (2022) → $20B (2023) = ~50% jump. Likely earlier figure was understated. Newer estimates more reliable.

### Confidence level
- **Medium-High.** Multiple independent sources converge on $20B+ for 2023.
- **Range:** $22B (conservative) to $26B (aggressive).
- **Working figure:** $24B.

## Store Count: 608 (mid-2025)

### Sources
- Acquired Briefing: 608 stores across 43 states (2025)
- Progressive Grocer: opened 34 stores in 2024
- Implied 2024 year-end: ~595 stores

### Confidence
- **High.** Public business locator allows direct count.

## Employee Count: ~70,000

### Sources
- Acquired Briefing: 70,000 employees (across 608 stores ≈ 115 per store)
- Bullfincher: ~50,000 (likely older figure)
- IBISWorld: ~50,000

### Confidence
- **Medium.** Likely between 50K–70K. Use 70K as upper bound for current scale.

## Key Caveats
- TJ is owned by Aldi Nord (private German entity); files no public financials.
- All revenue estimates are second-hand; CEO Dan Bane has only loosely confirmed >$20B publicly.
- Per-store productivity ($24B ÷ 608 = $39.5M/store) sanity-checks against industry-reported $1,750–$2,100 sales/sqft × ~10,000 sqft typical TJ = $17–21M minimum. Higher implied figure ($39.5M) suggests stores may average closer to 15,000 sqft or sales/sqft is at upper bound.
